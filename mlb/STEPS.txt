GOODMAN / TOP-OF-BOARD — what I changed and why
================================================

THE FINDING (why I did NOT nerf him):
  You were right that he's annoying at the top — but the cause is NOT miscalibration.
  - He is NOT #1 every day. On the road (park -14%) the model correctly buries him:
    14th, 16th, 20th, 26th on the 07-09..07-12 trip. Park discrimination works.
  - What you see is a Coors homestand (07-17 on): ranks 3,4,3,1. It recurs every homestand.
  - I tested Coors/high-HR parks on the FULL 25,128-prediction backtest:
        projected 12.0%  ->  actual 11.7%   gap -0.3   (2SE +/-0.9)  = DEAD CALIBRATED
    The -4.3 Coors gap in the last 13 days was a COLD STREAK, not a bias.
  - Goodman's 1-for-8 is a coin flip for a correctly-projected ~24% bat (39-50% odds).
  => Shading Coors down would take a calibrated tier and make it UNDER-project. So I didn't.
     "Top only on days he homers" is an oracle no model has — a correct 31% still misses 2 of 3.

THE FIX (readability, not probability):
  Added a "spot" layer: each bat's today HR% vs its OWN recent median (last 8 games).
  A perennial Coors name now reads STANDOUT only on genuinely elevated days vs his floor.
    - Goodman today: 31.1% vs own 23% -> +7.9 -> STANDOUT (soft LHP Palmquist, wind out 7, hot pen)
    - On a floor Coors day he'd show ~0 edge and read normal.
  hr_pct and fair are UNTOUCHED — the calibrated numbers don't move.
  Selective by design: on today's board = 2 STANDOUT, 1 floor, 22 normal, 5 new.

  2. Each hr_board.json row gains: base_self, edge_self, spot.
  3. hr_predictions.csv gains two columns: edge_self, spot.
